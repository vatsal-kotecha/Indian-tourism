PUSHKAR LAKE


Immerse yourself in India's religious heritage and humanitarian culture! Take a tour to Pushkar and witness the important Hindu pilgrimage sites. This small town is considered as a melange of religion, culture and scenic beauty. Often regarded as "tirth- raj" which means king of pilgrimages, it is believed that every devotee should visit this holy site at least once in a lifetime. Pushkar has a magnetism of its own! It attracts large no of disciples from all over the world as it houses the most sacred, Brahma temple.
A town in the renowned district of Ajmer, Rajasthan, Pushkar is known for its religious and tourism-related activities. Snuggled on the footsteps of the Aravalli hills, it is one of the ancient cities in India. The spiritual atmosphere, beautiful lake, wide street lanes, colourful fairs, and festivals, make a tourist fall in love with this place. Known as the "rose garden of Rajasthan", Pushkar exports roses in large numbers. Every year, Pushkar tourism arranges a bunch of interesting cultural activities to woo tourists. Pushkar mela is organized every year in the month of Kartik, and one can witness many un-usual activities like camel dancing, bump riding, neck shaking, camel race, tattoo making, Matka Phod, moustache, bridal competition, etc. It is an unparalleled and unforgettable fair which captures the essence and colourful elegance of entire Rajasthan.


Brahma Temple

0 kms from city center.
Brahma Sarovar is an ancient water tank sacred to Hinduism in Thanesar, in the state of Haryana, North India.
Hinduism stresses on having a bath in the sarovar for internal and external sanctity, purity and bliss forever. Most religious places have water tanks or sarovars in the vicinity of temple or gurdwara. The temple is one of very few existing temples dedicated to the Hindu creator-god Brahma in India and remains the most prominent among them.
Although the present temple structure dates to the 14th century, the temple is believed to be 2000 years old. The temple is mainly built of marble and stone stabs. It has a distinct red pinnacle (shikhara) and a hamsa bird motif. The temple sanctum sanctorum holds the central images of Brahma and his second consort Gayatri.

Pushkar Cattle Fair

0 kms from city center.
The annual five-day camel and livestock fair, held at the bank of Pushkar Lake in the town of Pushkar is one of the world's largest camel fairs. Apart from buying and selling of livestock it has become an important tourist attraction as there are various competitions like "matka phod", "longest moustache", and "bridal competition" which attract thousands of tourists.
Thousands of people go to the banks of the Pushkar Lake where the fair takes place. Men buy and sell their livestock, which includes camels, cows, sheep and goats.
The women go to the stalls, full of bracelets, clothes, textiles and fabrics. A camel race starts off the festival, with music, songs and exhibitions to follow. Between these events, the most waited for is the test of how the camel is able to bring the items.

Varaha Temple

0 kms from city center.
The largest and the most ancient temple of Pushkar, Varaha Temple is dedicated to Varaha The Boar which is the third incarnation (avatar) of Lord Vishnu.
The Varaha temple houses an image of lord Vishnu in the incarnation of wild boar. Varaha slew the demon and retrieved the Earth from the ocean, lifting it on his tusks, and restored Bhudevi to her place in the universe.
Varaha may be depicted completely as a boar or in an anthropomorphic form, with a boar's head and human body. His consort, Bhudevi, the earth, is often depicted as a young woman, lifted by Varaha.
